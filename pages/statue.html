Statue of Zeus at Olympia

Overview The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a massive, seated statue of the god Zeus, created by the sculptor Phidias around 435 BCE for the Temple of Zeus in Olympia, Greece.
History The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, created around 435 BCE by the sculptor Phidias. Housed in the Temple of Zeus at Olympia, the colossal sculpture was made of ivory and gold-plated bronze, depicting Zeus on a throne.
Location Olympia, Elis region, Peloponnese, Greece
