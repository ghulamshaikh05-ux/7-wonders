Colosseum

Overview The Colosseum is the largest amphitheatre ever built, a symbol of the power and engineering skill of ancient Rome.
History Commissioned by Emperor Vespasian and inaugurated in AD 80 by Titus, it hosted gladiatorial contests and spectacles for up to 50,000 people. Despite damage from earthquakes, it stands as one of Rome’s greatest monuments.
Location Rome, Italy — historic city center
