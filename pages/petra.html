Petra

Overview Petra, the “Rose City,” is famed for its rock-cut tombs and temples carved into pink sandstone cliffs, reached through a narrow gorge called the Siq.
History Founded by the Nabataeans in the 4th century BC, Petra thrived as a trade hub linking Arabia, Egypt, and the Mediterranean. After earthquakes and shifting trade routes, it was abandoned and rediscovered in 1812 by Johann Burckhardt.
Location Ma’an Governorate, Southern Jordan
