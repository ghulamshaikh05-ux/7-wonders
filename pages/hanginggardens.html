Hanging Gardens of Babylon

Overview The Hanging Gardens of Babylon are described as magnificent terraced gardens filled with exotic plants, said to have been built in the ancient city of Babylon.
History Though their existence remains debated, ancient writers described them as built by King Nebuchadnezzar II around 600 BC for his queen. If true, they represented a remarkable feat of engineering and irrigation in the desert.
Location Ancient Babylon, near modern Hillah, Iraq
