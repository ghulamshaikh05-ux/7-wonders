Mount Everest

Overview Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth, located in the Himalayas on the border of Nepal and China. Its summit is approximately 8,849 meters (29,032 feet) above sea level. Known locally as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, it was named after Sir George Everest in 1865.
History Mount Everest's history includes its geological formation and a 19th-century naming by British surveyors, who first identified it as the world's tallest peak.
