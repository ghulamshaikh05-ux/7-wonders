Chichen Itza

Overview Chichen Itza was one of the largest and most important cities of the Maya civilization. Its impressive structures, such as El Castillo, reveal the Maya’s deep knowledge of astronomy and architecture.
History Built between the 7th and 10th centuries, the city served as a major center for politics, trade, and religion. The Temple of Kukulcán aligns with the equinox sun, creating a serpent-shaped shadow — a masterpiece of ancient design.
Location Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico
