Lighthouse of Alexandria

Overview The Lighthouse of Alexandria, also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, was an ancient wonder that guided sailors safely into Egypt’s bustling harbor.
History Built during the reign of Ptolemy II around the 3rd century BC, the lighthouse stood over 100 meters tall. It was severely damaged by earthquakes and eventually disappeared, but remains one of the most influential architectural designs in history.
Location Alexandria, Egypt — Pharos Island, near the city’s harbor
