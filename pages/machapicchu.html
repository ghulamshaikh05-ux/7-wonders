Machu Picchu

Overview Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel set high in the Andes Mountains. Its terraced fields and stone temples blend seamlessly into the surrounding peaks.
History Built around 1450 under Emperor Pachacuti, it likely served as a royal estate or religious site. Hidden from the Spanish and rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham, it remains a masterpiece of Inca engineering and spirituality.
