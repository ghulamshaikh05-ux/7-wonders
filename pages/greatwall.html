Great Wall of China

Overview The Great Wall of China is a vast system of fortifications stretching thousands of kilometers across northern China. It was built to protect Chinese states from invasions and to mark imperial boundaries.
History Construction began as early as the 7th century BC and continued for centuries, reaching its greatest extent under the Ming Dynasty (14th–17th centuries). Built of stone, brick, and earth, it stands today as a symbol of Chinese ingenuity and endurance.
Location Northern China — across several provinces from Gansu to Hebei
